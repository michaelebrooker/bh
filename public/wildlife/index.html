Buhooro is in a World Heritage protected Bird and Wildlife wild life area, and commonly seen species of birds are:

  • Egrets, fish eagle, Ibis, Shoebill, Egyption Geese, pied kingfisher, weaver birds, Thick Knees, cranes

  • Otters live in the lake.

Reptiles: Monitor lizards forage for birds eggs, which some aquatic birds lay on the ground, such Thick Knees. Shellfish: the lake is rich in fresh water shellfish, such as crabs and many other mollusces Fish: Populations of fish introduced in the 19th Century during the Colonial period do dominate the marine fauna. Tilapia, Nile Perch, lungfish and whitebait, called locally Mukene. Please note that fishing by guests from the beach is not permitted by the Lake fishing authority.
